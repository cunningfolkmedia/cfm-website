# Cunning Folk Jacket-Copy Framework

## Purpose

This framework exists to make Cunning Folk jacket copy feel like part of a disciplined list rather than a random collection of titles. Every back cover, retailer description, catalog listing, and launch blurb should sound like it belongs to the same house while preserving the unique voltage of the individual title.

The goal is not just to summarize a book. The goal is to make the right reader feel the book was built for them and to make the house standard visible in the first paragraph.

## The Core Principles

A Cunning Folk jacket must do six jobs:

1. **Classify the book fast.** What kind of book is this?
2. **Name the tension.** What problem, contradiction, obsession, or force is driving it?
3. **Establish consequence.** Why does this matter beyond “interesting subject”? 
4. **Signal the house standard.** Show that this book is serious, not category sludge.
5. **Select the reader.** Make the ideal reader feel recognized.
6. **Create desire without hype.** The copy should generate urgency, not desperation.

## The Core Formula

At its strongest, a Cunning Folk jacket follows this sequence:

### 1. The Hook
A direct, high-pressure opening line that names the book and its central claim, conflict, or field of intervention.

### 2. The Expansion
A paragraph that explains what the book actually does and what makes it distinct.

### 3. The Stakes
A paragraph that answers why this book matters to the reader, the field, or the moment.

### 4. The Reader Lock
A line or short paragraph that makes the intended reader feel directly addressed.

### 5. The House-Grade Finish
A final sentence with consequence, voltage, or editorial force.

Not every book needs the same paragraph lengths, but the underlying movement should stay consistent.

## Length Targets

### Back Cover Copy
150–230 words

### Retailer Description / Product Page Copy
180–320 words

### Catalog Copy
80–140 words

### Short Ad / Social Launch Copy
20–60 words

Use the same conceptual spine across all versions. Do not write four unrelated blurbs for the same book.

## The Opening Sentence Rules

The first sentence matters more than the rest of the copy put together.

It should:
- name the category or type of intervention
- establish force
- avoid throat-clearing
- avoid generic praise language
- make a real claim

### Weak openings
- “In this fascinating and timely book…”
- “This groundbreaking work explores…”
- “For centuries, humans have wondered…”
- “This compelling volume offers a unique journey…”

These are dead on arrival.

### Strong openings
- “A fierce history of ritual technology for readers tired of occultism without context and scholarship without life.”
- “Part grimoire, part cultural diagnosis, this book argues that digital systems are not merely tools but environments of enchantment.”
- “A dark, contemporary fairy tale cycle in which magic is never metaphor for long.”

These openings classify and provoke at once.

## Copy Modes by Category

## 1. Nonfiction — Babalon Press

### Core qualities to signal
- specific intervention
- intellectual seriousness
- operational credibility
- historical depth
- clear stakes

### Structure

**Sentence 1:** Name the book’s field and pressure point.

**Paragraph 1:** What is the book doing that others in the category fail to do?

**Paragraph 2:** What problem does it solve for the serious reader? What does it recover, argue, map, or expose?

**Closing line:** Why does it matter now, in this house, for this audience?

### Template

`[Title]` is a `[adjective]` `[type of book]` on `[subject]` for readers who want `[desire]` without `[failure mode]`.

Moving between `[domain A]` and `[domain B]`, it `[verb phrase describing intervention]` and shows `[reader payoff]`.

For practitioners, historians, theorists, and rigorously open readers alike, this is a book about `[stakes]`: not just what the material says, but what it does, what it assumes, and what survives when it is put to use.

`[Final line with voltage.]`

### Example skeleton

*The Grammar of Spirits* is a hard, lucid study of spirit contact for readers who want more than inherited lore and less than dead academic distance.

Drawing on ritual practice, textual history, and close conceptual analysis, it asks what practitioners mean when they speak of spirits, what traditions built those meanings, and what gets lost when the subject is reduced to psychology on one side or pious assertion on the other.

For readers who take the material seriously enough to demand better language for it, this is not another book of correspondences. It is a book about contact, consequence, and the disciplines required to think clearly at the threshold.

## 2. Fiction — FOLK Fiction

### Core qualities to signal
- atmosphere with consequence
- premise with symbolic depth
- style and emotional register
- stakes in the story world
- the nature of the strange element

### Structure

**Sentence 1:** Introduce the premise with force.

**Paragraph 1:** Establish the story world and the pressure on the protagonist or ensemble.

**Paragraph 2:** Expand the strange element and why it matters emotionally, morally, spiritually, or socially.

**Closing line:** Promise a reading experience, not just plot continuation.

### Template

In `[setting or condition]`, `[protagonist]` must `[core conflict]` when `[strange element]` begins to `[effect]`.

As `[stakes escalate]`, the story opens into `[larger thematic field]`: `[ritual / grief / power / myth / haunting / desire / corruption / revelation]`.

`[Title]` is a `[tone adjectives]` novel / collection about `[central forces]`, written for readers who want `[reader payoff]` without `[genre failure mode]`.

`[Final line with atmosphere and consequence.]`

### Example skeleton

When a failed ritual leaves a crack in the architecture of her city, Mara discovers that every apology, prayer, and unpaid debt is beginning to take physical form.

What starts as private damage becomes civic contagion. As old symbols wake inside new systems, she is forced to choose between repairing the wound and becoming the kind of woman who can live inside it.

*Ashes & Algorithms* is a dark contemporary novel of ritual, memory, and technological haunting for readers who want magic with consequence and fiction with an actual soul in it.

## 3. Editorial / Essay Collections — FOLK Magazine or crossover titles

### Core qualities to signal
- argument or conceptual frame
- topical or cultural relevance
- voice and intelligence
- angle, not just subject
- likely overlap audience

### Structure

**Sentence 1:** State the argument or conceptual hook.

**Paragraph 1:** Show the terrain covered by the book or collection.

**Paragraph 2:** Explain why the framing matters now and for whom.

**Closing line:** Promise a kind of reading experience.

### Template

What if `[cultural phenomenon]` is not a distraction from `[deeper issue]` but one of its clearest expressions?

In `[Title]`, `[author]` moves through `[list of terrains]` to argue that `[main claim]`.

Written for readers of `[overlap references if appropriate]` and anyone tired of choosing between cultural criticism and esoteric seriousness, this is a book of `[qualities]`: sharp, strange, historically alert, and unwilling to flatter the reader into passivity.

`[Final line with intellectual and emotional charge.]`

## The House Signature Moves

These are the moves that make Cunning Folk copy feel like Cunning Folk copy.

### 1. Clean opposition
Build the book’s need by naming what it is not.

Examples:
- not another beginner manual
- not a bloodless academic history
- not occult décor in novel form
- not criticism embarrassed by the spiritual

### 2. Reader recognition
Let the right buyer feel selected.

Examples:
- for readers tired of choosing between shallow occultism and dead scholarship
- for readers past the beginner shelf
- for practitioners, historians, and rigorously open readers alike
- for readers who want magic with consequence

### 3. Consequence language
Use nouns that imply real stakes.

Examples:
- pressure
- cost
- consequence
- contact
- rupture
- appetite
- threshold
- system
- transmission
- aftermath
- force

### 4. Controlled voltage
End with a sentence that leaves a mark.

Examples:
- This is a book about what survives at the threshold.
- The result is not comfort but clarity.
- It does not explain away the strange. It enters it under pressure.
- This is where the real argument begins.

## Words to Favor in Jacket Copy

- fierce
- lucid
- exacting
- charged
- hard-won
- dark
- living
- serious
- unsentimental
- symbolically rich
- historically informed
- operationally serious
- intellectually alive

Use these words when they are true. They work because they imply standards.

## Words to Avoid in Jacket Copy

- fascinating
- enlightening
- unique
- groundbreaking
- transformative
- accessible to everyone
- timeless wisdom
- mysterious journey
- captivating exploration
- hidden truths revealed
- spirituality for modern life

Most of these are category clichés or generic marketing filler.

## The Back Cover Blueprint

For a standard Cunning Folk back cover, use this order:

### Top third
A hard opening paragraph with the hook and intervention.

### Middle third
A paragraph explaining the material, stakes, or story field.

### Bottom third
A short lock-on line for the intended reader, followed by one final sentence with current.

### Optional components
- one sharp endorsement line
- one short author bio block
- imprint mark and category line

Do not overcrowd the back cover. A strong 170 words beats a flabby 260 every time.

## Retailer Description Blueprint

Retail copy should be a slightly expanded version of the jacket, not a new personality.

Include:
- a clean opening hook
- a second paragraph on scope and distinctiveness
- a third paragraph on audience and stakes
- optional short bullet list only when platform norms demand scanability

Use bullets sparingly. Cunning Folk should not look like it sells productivity software.

## Endorsement Selection Rules

Choose endorsements that reinforce one or more of these:
- seriousness
- originality
- conceptual clarity
- literary force
- operational credibility

Avoid endorsements that sound like generic blurber inflation.

Weak:
- “A must-read!”
- “Brilliant and insightful.”
- “A fascinating journey.”

Stronger:
- “One of the few recent books in the field willing to think with the material rather than merely around it.”
- “A rare fusion of operational literacy and historical discipline.”
- “Dark, exact, and symbolically alive.”

## Subtitle Rules

Subtitles should do classification work. They should not merely decorate the title.

Useful subtitle structures:
- **A History of…**
- **Essays on…**
- **Ritual, Technology, and…**
- **Modern Tales of…**
- **Occult Theory for…**
- **Magic, Media, and…**

Avoid subtitles that sound like SEO mush or spiritual self-help.

## Audience Lock Lines

Keep a bank of these. They are useful across jackets, ads, catalogs, and retailer pages.

- For readers past the beginner shelf.
- For practitioners who demand better books.
- For readers who take the material seriously enough to argue with it.
- For the rigorously open.
- For readers tired of choosing between shallow occultism and dead scholarship.
- For anyone who wants criticism sharp enough to survive contact with the weird.
- For readers who want fiction with a living current in it.

## Failure Modes to Watch For

### 1. Summary without position
If the copy only tells us what the book contains, it is not enough.

### 2. Hype without substance
If the copy sounds louder but tells us less, it has failed.

### 3. Category mimicry
If the book sounds like it could have been published by any New Age or general occult press, it is off-brand.

### 4. House voice collapse
If the copy sounds like a university press, a lifestyle brand, or an Amazon keyword farm, it is wrong.

### 5. Over-classification
If the copy becomes a taxonomy lesson, it loses force.

## Quick Revision Checklist

Before finalizing jacket copy, ask:

- Does the first sentence make a real claim?
- Is the book’s difference legible by paragraph one?
- Have we named the intended reader clearly enough?
- Does the copy carry the house standard?
- Is there at least one sentence with voltage?
- Could a competitor say this exact copy about a weaker book? If yes, sharpen it.

## Sample Modular Formula Bank

Use these fragments as building blocks.

### Hook fragments
- A hard, lucid history of…
- A dark contemporary novel of…
- An exacting study of…
- A fierce collection of essays on…
- Part ritual manual, part cultural diagnosis…
- A book for readers tired of…

### Distinction fragments
- without flattening the material into metaphor
- without surrendering rigor to mystique
- written from the point where archive meets altar
- for readers who expect more than category sludge
- with the force of practice and the discipline of history

### Stakes fragments
- because the language we use for this material changes what we can do with it
- because not every tradition survives simplification intact
- because the strange is always easiest to mishandle when it becomes fashionable
- because fiction is one of the last places the numinous can still arrive without apology
- because smart readers deserve better books than this field usually gives them

### Finish fragments
- This is where the real argument begins.
- The result is not reassurance but clarity.
- It does not explain away the strange. It enters it under pressure.
- Built for readers who prefer consequence to comfort.
- A book meant to be used, argued with, and kept.

## Final Rule

Every Cunning Folk jacket should feel like the book has already survived an editorial fight and won on merit.

If the copy sounds merely pleasant, inclusive, or market-correct, it is not ready.
